About the Community Forest

Since the 1990s, Campbell's Bay Primary School has nurtured a regenerating native forest - a living classroom where students connect with New Zealand's natural heritage. What began as overgrown land is now home to tūī, kererū, and thriving native species.

Who was Involved?

Here are some of the volunteers and sponsors who helped to bring massive change to the Community Forest.

Fiona George

In 1996 the CBS Board of Trustees started a regeneration project for the forest. This is when volunteer Fiona George (a parent of the school) developed the management plan for the project.

Richard Hursthouse

Also a parent of the school, Richard Hursthouse played a massive part in helping guide other volunteers in actioning this plan.

He is now with the North Shore Forest & Bird and habitat restoration for Centennial Park, though he continues to give time and guideance for the forest

About this Website

The creation of this website was for a university assignment (at Whitecliffe University) about bringing positive social change to a community. I choose the Community Forest as I was a past student of Campbell's Bay Primary School, where the forest is located. With the support from the schools' teachers and parents from the school (both past and present), I was able to come up with some designs which would futher promote learning about the Community Forest.

~ Liv Broughton
